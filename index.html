1. Introduction

This portfolio analyzes a selected diary entry by Chinese author Fang Fang from March 3, 2020, which is part of her "Wuhan Diary". The aim is to contextualize the text historically, socially, and politically through accompanying materials.

2. Source

Fang Fang: Too Many People and Things Ignored. Caixin Blog, March 3, 2020.
URL: caixinglobal.com

3. Summary

Fang Fang describes the grim atmosphere in Wuhan, reactions to new case numbers, political consequences of outbreaks in prisons, and the suffering of patients and medical staff. She recalls the suicide of Chang Kai, calls for journalistic investigation, and criticizes the disregard for volunteers and migrant workers.

4. Contextual Materials

  • Weather: Rainy, 10°C (Wunderground archive)
  • COVID-19 Situation: 196 new cases, including 233 in prisons (WHO Report, March 3, 2020)
  • Chang Kai: Suicide due to lack of medical care (The Guardian)
  • Zeng Guang quote: "How could I have known ..." – reflects on official responsibility
  • Volunteers: Wang Yong, Wu You – reported by SCMP & Rest of World
  • Migrant Workers: Return-to-work appeals circulated via WeChat (China Digital Times)

5. Analysis

The entry from March 3, 2020 focuses on the tension between collective overload and individual responsibility. Fang Fang’s language shifts between documentary sobriety and personal empathy. Her critique is subtle but firm: the state was overwhelmed while individuals stepped up. The contrast between governmental passivity and civic initiative runs throughout the entry.

“Too many people and things have been ignored.” – Fang Fang

6. Conclusion

The analyzed entry exemplifies Fang Fang’s diary project, interweaving personal experience, systemic critique, and cultural reflection. The additional materials confirm many of her observations and underscore her role as a critical voice during an exceptional period.
